About Marvel

Marvel Studios has created a cinematic universe unlike any other, filled with legendary heroes, villains, and thrilling adventures. From Iron Man's groundbreaking debut in 2008 to the epic finale of Avengers: Endgame, Marvel has redefined how we experience superhero storytelling. Marvel Studios releases its films in groups called "Phases", with the first three phases collectively known as "The Infinity Saga" and the following three phases as "The Multiverse Saga". The first MCU film, Iron Man (2008), began Phase One, which culminated in the 2012 crossover film The Avengers. Phase Two began with Iron Man 3 (2013) and concluded with Ant-Man (2015), while Phase Three began with Captain America: Civil War (2016) and concluded with Spider-Man: Far From Home (2019). Black Widow (2021) is the first film in Phase Four, which concluded with Black Panther: Wakanda Forever (2022), while Phase Five began with Ant-Man and the Wasp: Quantumania (2023) and will conclude with Thunderbolts* (2025). Phase Six will begin with The Fantastic Four: First Steps (2025) and it will conclude with Avengers: Doomsday (2026) and Avengers: Secret Wars (2027).

Featured Marvel Movies

Iron Man

"Iron Man" is the groundbreaking film that kicked off the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). It follows Tony Stark, a genius billionaire and weapons manufacturer, who is captured by terrorists. Using his intellect and resources, Stark builds a powerful suit of armor to escape and ultimately becomes the armored hero, Iron Man. The film explores themes of redemption, responsibility, and the consequences of unchecked power. Robert Downey Jr.'s charismatic portrayal of Tony Stark made him an iconic character, setting the tone for the expansive MCU that would follow.

Avengers: Endgame

"Avengers: Endgame" is the epic conclusion to the Marvel Cinematic Universe's Infinity Saga. After the devastating events of "Infinity War," where Thanos wiped out half of all life in the universe, the remaining Avengers and their allies must come together to undo the destruction. With time travel and incredible sacrifices, they confront Thanos in a final battle to restore balance and save the universe. The film is a culmination of over a decade of storytelling, filled with emotional moments, heroic sacrifices, and thrilling action, marking the end of an era for the Avengers.

Black Panther

"Black Panther" tells the story of T'Challa, the newly crowned king of Wakanda, a technologically advanced and secretive African nation. After the death of his father, T'Challa must step up to lead his people while facing the threat of Killmonger, a vengeful adversary with ties to his family. As T'Challa struggles with his responsibilities as king and his identity as Black Panther, the film explores themes of heritage, leadership, and global responsibility. Praised for its cultural significance and stunning visuals, "Black Panther" became a groundbreaking film in both the superhero genre and Hollywood.
